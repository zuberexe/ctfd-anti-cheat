Investigation Checklist

Before acting on a flag, sanity-check the following — most false positives die in this list:

  • Is the user on a shared corporate / university / household NAT? Look at the IP overlap detail and check whether the "other" accounts in the bucket are friends/family who registered with similar names or addresses.
  • Does the user have organic failed attempts before their solves? (Failure ratio above ≥ 0.4 strongly suggests real problem-solving.)
  • Are the simultaneous-solve hits all on the same trivial challenge that several people happened to solve in the closing minutes?
  • Is the "identical wrong flag" the actual flag format like flag{} or CTF{test} — a common placeholder rather than copied work?
  • Did the suspect actually have admin contact (Discord/email) during the event window? Sometimes "rare clean solve" = "they asked a good clarifying question and got hint-level info."
  • Cross-reference with chat / scoreboard chatter logs if you have them. Sudden surges often correlate with public hint releases.
